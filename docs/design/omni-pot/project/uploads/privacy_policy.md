## 3. How Omni Pot Processes Content

### 3.1 Local Processing

Some features may run locally on your device, such as local configuration storage, local history storage, local backup, local dictionaries, local OCR where available, system text-to-speech, and local HTTP API automation.

For local-only processing, content does not need to be sent to us.

### 3.2 Default Online Translation Services

Omni Pot may enable Google Translate and Bing Translate by default and may allow translation without requiring you to provide your own API key.

When you use these online translation services, the text you submit for translation may be sent to the relevant provider for processing. These providers may process data according to their own terms and privacy policies.

Do not translate sensitive or confidential text using online services unless you are comfortable with that content being sent to the provider.

### 3.3 Other Third-Party Services

Omni Pot may also support third-party translation, OCR, dictionary, text-to-speech, AI, and cloud services.

When you enable or use a third-party service, content may be sent to that provider, including:

* text for translation;
* screenshots or images for OCR or AI vision processing;
* words or phrases for dictionary lookup;
* text for text-to-speech;
* service configuration or API credentials needed to authenticate requests.

Third-party services are governed by their own privacy policies and terms. We do not control their data practices.

### 3.4 Clipboard Monitoring

Clipboard monitoring is optional.

If you enable clipboard monitoring, Omni Pot may monitor clipboard text and trigger translation when new text is detected.

Do not enable clipboard monitoring if your clipboard may contain passwords, private messages, financial information, business secrets, or other sensitive information that you do not want processed.

### 3.5 Screenshot Capture and Text Recognition

If you use screenshot capture, text recognition, or screenshot translation, Omni Pot may process selected screen regions, images, recognized text, and translated text.

Depending on the selected OCR or translation service, screenshots, images, recognized text, or translated text may be processed locally or sent to third-party services.

You are responsible for ensuring that you have permission to capture and process content displayed on your screen.

### 3.6 Local HTTP API

Omni Pot may provide a local HTTP API for local automation.

The local API is intended for use on your own device. You are responsible for protecting the local API token and ensuring that scripts or tools using the API are trusted.
