This Privacy Policy explains how information is collected, used, stored, and shared when you use Omni Pot, our website, our account or license services, and related support services.

By using Omni Pot, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

1Summary

Omni Pot is primarily a desktop application. Many app features process data locally on your device.

However, some features may use our servers, Paddle, website analytics tools, email tools, or third-party translation, OCR, dictionary, text-to-speech, AI, and cloud services.

Important points:

  • We operate a website and server-side services for accounts, licensing, downloads, updates, support, and related product functions.
  • We do not automatically upload app logs, telemetry, or crash reports from the desktop app to our servers.
  • If you contact support, you may choose to send logs, screenshots, text, or diagnostic information to us.
  • By default, Omni Pot may use online services such as Google Translate and Bing Translate. Text sent to those services may be processed by those providers.
  • Payments may be processed by Paddle. We do not store your full payment card details.
  • We may use website analytics and may send product or marketing emails where permitted by law.

2Information We Collect or Process

2.1 Account and License Information

If you create an account, activate a license, access downloads, or use server-based features, we may process:

  • name or display name, if provided;
  • email address;
  • account credentials or authentication tokens;
  • license key or license status;
  • order ID or purchase reference;
  • subscription or plan status;
  • activation, login, or access records;
  • approximate region or country;
  • support and account communication history.

2.2 Payment and Order Information

Purchases may be processed by Paddle.

We do not collect or store your full payment card number. Paddle may process payment details, billing information, tax information, transaction records, refund requests, invoices, and related order information according to Paddle’s own terms and privacy policy.

We may receive limited order-related information from Paddle, such as:

  • buyer email;
  • order ID;
  • product purchased;
  • payment status;
  • refund status;
  • subscription status;
  • currency;
  • country or region;
  • tax-related information where applicable;
  • transaction metadata needed for licensing, support, fraud prevention, accounting, and compliance.

2.3 Website Information

When you visit https://zzzkkkccc.site, we may process:

  • IP address;
  • browser type and version;
  • device type;
  • operating system;
  • referring page;
  • pages visited;
  • approximate location derived from IP address;
  • analytics events;
  • form submissions;
  • newsletter or marketing email preferences.

We may use analytics tools to understand website traffic, product interest, and website performance.

2.4 Cookies and Similar Technologies

We may use cookies and similar technologies on our website. These may include:

  • Strictly necessary cookies: required for the website to function, such as session management and security cookies. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: used to understand how visitors use our website, such as page views, traffic sources, and session duration.
  • Preference cookies: used to remember your settings, such as language or region.

Where required by applicable law, we will display a cookie consent mechanism that allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies before they are placed on your device.

You may also manage cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality.

Cookie retention periods vary by type. Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies may last up to 12 months unless you delete them earlier.

2.5 Support Information

If you contact us for support, we may process:

  • your email address;
  • your name or display name;
  • order or license information;
  • support messages;
  • screenshots, logs, files, or diagnostic information you choose to provide;
  • information about your operating system, app version, device environment, and issue.

Please review any logs, screenshots, or files before sending them to us, because they may contain text, screenshots, OCR content, configuration details, or other sensitive information.

2.6 Content Processed in the App

Depending on how you use Omni Pot, the Software may process:

  • text you type, select, copy, or translate;
  • clipboard text, if clipboard monitoring is enabled;
  • screenshots or selected screen regions;
  • images used for text recognition;
  • OCR results;
  • translation results;
  • dictionary lookup terms and results;
  • text-to-speech requests;
  • local HTTP API requests;
  • translation history;
  • app settings;
  • service configuration and service credentials.

Some of this content may remain local on your device. Some content may be sent to third-party services depending on the feature and service used.

2.7 Local Configuration, History, Logs, and Backups

Omni Pot may store local data on your device, including:

  • app settings;
  • service settings;
  • local API token;
  • translation history, unless disabled;
  • local dictionary data;
  • logs;
  • backup files;
  • service credentials or API keys, if configured;
  • update-related data.

Local data remains on your device unless you choose to share it, back it up, send it to support, or use a feature that sends content to a third-party service.

3How Omni Pot Processes Content

3.1 Local Processing

Some features may run locally on your device, such as local configuration storage, local history storage, local backup, local dictionaries, local OCR where available, system text-to-speech, and local HTTP API automation.

For local-only processing, content does not need to be sent to us.

3.2 Default Online Translation Services

Omni Pot may enable Google Translate and Bing Translate by default and may allow translation without requiring you to provide your own API key.

When you use these online translation services, the text you submit for translation may be sent to the relevant provider for processing. These providers may process data according to their own terms and privacy policies.

Do not translate sensitive or confidential text using online services unless you are comfortable with that content being sent to the provider.

3.3 Other Third-Party Services

Omni Pot may also support third-party translation, OCR, dictionary, text-to-speech, AI, and cloud services.

When you enable or use a third-party service, content may be sent to that provider, including:

  • text for translation;
  • screenshots or images for OCR or AI vision processing;
  • words or phrases for dictionary lookup;
  • text for text-to-speech;
  • service configuration or API credentials needed to authenticate requests.

Third-party services are governed by their own privacy policies and terms. We do not control their data practices.

3.4 Clipboard Monitoring

Clipboard monitoring is optional.

If you enable clipboard monitoring, Omni Pot may monitor clipboard text and trigger translation when new text is detected.

Do not enable clipboard monitoring if your clipboard may contain passwords, private messages, financial information, business secrets, or other sensitive information that you do not want processed.

3.5 Screenshot Capture and Text Recognition

If you use screenshot capture, text recognition, or screenshot translation, Omni Pot may process selected screen regions, images, recognized text, and translated text.

Depending on the selected OCR or translation service, screenshots, images, recognized text, or translated text may be processed locally or sent to third-party services.

You are responsible for ensuring that you have permission to capture and process content displayed on your screen.

3.6 Local HTTP API

Omni Pot may provide a local HTTP API for local automation.

The local API is intended for use on your own device. You are responsible for protecting the local API token and ensuring that scripts or tools using the API are trusted.

4No Automatic Log, Telemetry, or Crash Uploads from the Desktop App

The desktop app does not automatically upload logs, telemetry, or crash reports to us.

Logs may be stored locally on your device for troubleshooting. If you contact support, you may choose to send logs or diagnostic files to us. We will process those files only for support, debugging, security, or product improvement purposes.

  • provide, operate, maintain, and improve Omni Pot;
  • create and manage accounts;
  • deliver downloads, licenses, updates, and paid features;
  • process purchases, subscriptions, refunds, invoices, and tax-related records;
  • provide customer support;
  • troubleshoot bugs, compatibility issues, and service problems;
  • protect account, license, payment, and product security;
  • prevent fraud, abuse, unauthorized sharing, or license misuse;
  • analyze website performance and product interest;
  • send product updates, service messages, support replies, and marketing emails where permitted;
  • comply with legal, accounting, tax, and regulatory obligations;
  • enforce our Terms of Service and protect legal rights.

We may still send non-marketing messages, such as purchase confirmations, account notices, license notices, security notices, support replies, and important product or policy updates.

Where applicable data protection laws require a legal basis, we process personal data based on one or more of the following:

We may share information with:

  • third-party translation, OCR, dictionary, text-to-speech, AI, and cloud services that you use through Omni Pot;

We do not sell your personal information.

9Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy. Typical retention periods include:

  • Account and license information: retained for the duration of your account or license and up to 3 years after the last activity or account closure, unless a longer period is required for legal compliance, fraud prevention, or dispute resolution;
  • Payment and order information: retained for up to 5 years to comply with tax, accounting, and regulatory obligations;
  • Support records: retained for up to 2 years after the last interaction, unless needed for ongoing issues or legal compliance;
  • Website analytics data: retained for up to 26 months in aggregated or anonymized form;
  • Marketing email preferences: retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion;
  • Server logs: retained for up to 90 days unless needed for security investigation.

Local app data remains on your device until you delete it, clear it in the app, uninstall the Software, or remove related files manually.

Paddle and third-party providers may retain data according to their own policies.
